## Day 1: Thursday 10 July (Arrival, Mt Wellington and Southern Hobart)

Get up early. Dad drives to the airport for the **6:30 AM flight**. Land in Hobart at **9:10 AM** then pick up the rental car at **10:00 AM**.

**Morning/Afternoon: Mt Wellington (11:00 AM to 1:30 PM)**

Drive straight up the mountain (about 30 minutes from the airport). Stop at The Springs for a coffee and a quick walk, then continue to The Pinnacle for the panoramic views.

Before heading up, check the BOM weather or the Mt Wellington webcam. If it's clouded over, swap this with the Huon Valley alternative below and save the mountain for Sunday.

**Cascades Area (2:00 PM)**

On the way back down the mountain, stop at:

- Big Bend
- The Chalet
- The Springs
- Fern Tree Park
- Cascade Brewery (the building itself is iconic)
- Cascades Female Factory (very moving history)

**Mt Nelson Signal Station (4:00 PM)**

Head here for a late afternoon view of the Derwent River. Free entry, less windy than the mountain, and has a great little tea house. Also a nice bit of free history worth soaking in.

**Evening**

Check into **Bay Hotel Apartments around 5:30 PM**, then grab dinner in the Hobart CBD or waterfront area. There are some great restaurants around the waterfront worth exploring.

_Optional: For something uniquely Tasmanian after dinner, visit Lark Distillery or Sullivan's Cove for a whisky tasting. Both are in central Hobart and make for a cosy winter evening._

### Alternative Day 1: Huon Valley Loop (if Mt Wellington is socked in)

If the mountain weather is no good, head south instead for a beautiful valley day.

- **Franklin (11:00 AM to 12:30 PM):** Drive south (about 45 minutes). Franklin is a riverside gem with historic buildings and traditional craftsmanship. Visit The Wooden Boat Centre for a guided tour where traditional wooden boats are handcrafted. Great on a cold day.
- **Glen Huon Dairy Co. (12:30 PM):** A short drive from Franklin in Glen Huon. Perfect for those who appreciate high-quality ingredients. Great for lunch or a tasting.
- **Cygnet (2:00 PM to 4:00 PM):** Take the scenic drive over the hill to Cygnet, known for its creative community and artisanal food. Stop at Cygnet Conservatory Cafe at the Cygnet Old Bank, a bright and welcoming spot in a beautifully restored historic bank building. Browse the artisan shops and soak up the village vibe.

Drive back to Hobart, check in, and grab dinner in the CBD.

## Day 2: Friday 11 July (Salamanca Market and Hobart Explorer)

**Salamanca Market (arrive by 8:30 AM)**

Hit the Saturday market with its 300 stalls of handmade Tasmanian pieces, collectibles, gourmet foods, jewellery, clothing, and farmers market produce. Grab a **curry scallop pie** while there, it's a uniquely Tasmanian thing. Browse until around **11:00 AM**.

**Late Morning**

Pick up a pastry at **Jackman & McRoss, 57 Hampden Rd, Battery Point**. It's a local favourite. Tasmania has an excellent cafe culture with great coffee everywhere, so don't rush past any good-looking cafe.

**Hop-On Hop-Off Bus (from 11:30 AM)**

Use the bus to explore Hobart at your own pace. Suggested stops:

- Brooke Street Pier
- Battery Point (wander the historic streets)
- Tasmanian Museum and Art Gallery (free)
- Maritime Museum of Tasmania
- **Hobart Convict Penitentiary** (**pre-book** this one, and absolutely DO NOT stand on the hanging trap door. You'll be thankful for that advice when you look under it later in the tour)
- Royal Tasmanian Botanical Gardens (free, and well worth the stop)
- Victoria Dock

You won't hit every stop in one day. Prioritise Battery Point for a walk, the Penitentiary tour, and the Botanical Gardens if time allows.

_Optional: Wrest Point Casino is also on the hop-on bus route if you want to pop in for a look._

**Evening**

Grab dinner around Salamanca Place or the Hobart waterfront. The food around Salamanca and the waterfront is some of the best in Hobart.

_Optional: Check "What's On Hobart" for any mid-July winter festival events happening that evening._

## Day 3: Saturday 12 July (Port Arthur and the Tasman Peninsula)

This is a big driving day. Start early to make the most of it.

- **8:00 AM:** Depart Hobart. It's roughly a 1.5 hour drive to Port Arthur.
- **9:00 AM: Dunalley Bakery.** Stop here for road trip snacks. Most country bakeries in Tassie do a good **curry scallop pie**, so grab one here if you missed out at the market.
- **10:00 AM: Eaglehawk Neck scenic loop.** Follow the tip to Cubes Coffee, and check out the Tessellated Pavement and the Blowhole. It's a quick, beautiful loop.
- **11:30 AM: Port Arthur Historic Site.** Allow at least **3-4 hours** to explore the ruins, gardens, and do the harbour cruise (included in the ticket). Aim to leave by **3:30 PM**.
- **4:00 PM: Coal River Farm** (634 Richmond Rd, Cambridge). On the drive back, stop for cheese and chocolate tasting.
- **4:45 PM: Richmond.** If there's still some daylight, swing through the town of Richmond to see the oldest bridge in Australia and have a quick walk around the village.
- **6:00 PM:** Back in Hobart for dinner.

**Booking reminder:** Port Arthur tickets and the Hobart Penitentiary tours fill up. **Book both online at least a week before** (by around **3 July**).

For more historic sites and tours, check the National Trust for Tasmania: https://www.nationaltrust.org.au/tas/

The best things in Tasmania are natural or historical, and this day has both in spades. Slow down and take it in rather than rushing through.

## Day 4: Sunday 13 July (Flex Day)

This is the unplanned day. Pick an option depending on energy and weather. Whatever the choice, start the morning with a proper cafe breakfast somewhere. Hobart's breakfast scene is genuinely excellent.

**Option A: Mt Wellington Make-Up**

If the Huon Valley was done on Day 1 because the mountain was clouded over, this is the chance. Do the mountain in the morning/early afternoon, then Mt Nelson Signal Station for late afternoon.

_Optional: If Mt Wellington is uncooperative again, head to Hastings Caves and Thermal Springs instead (about 1.5 hours south). It's a great all-weather alternative with guided cave tours and a warm thermal pool to soak in._

**Option B: Huon Valley and Cygnet**

If Mt Wellington was done on Day 1, today is the day for the Franklin, Glen Huon, and Cygnet loop (see the Alternative Day 1 section above).

**Option C: MONA and Relaxed Hobart Day**

This is the strongly recommended option if the energy is there. MONA (Museum of Old and New Art) is Tasmania's most famous attraction and a one-of-a-kind experience.

- Sleep in and enjoy a proper cafe breakfast
- Take the **ferry from Brooke Street Pier** (about 25 minutes) to MONA. Allow **3-4 hours** for the museum
- After MONA, revisit the Salamanca area shops without the market crowds
- Take a waterfront walk around Victoria Dock and Constitution Dock
- Check if the Lady Nelson is running harbour tours and get on the water if possible. Getting on the water is one of the best things to do in Hobart
- _Optional: A kayak or short cruise on the Derwent is another way to see Hobart from the water_

**Option D: Half-Day Heritage Sampler**

- Drive to **Richmond** (25 minutes from Hobart) for a proper explore of the bridge, gaol, and cafes
- Continue to **Bonorong Wildlife Sanctuary** (15 minutes further) for Tasmanian devils, wombats, and other native wildlife. This pairs really well with Richmond as a half-day trip
- _Optional: If there wasn't enough time in Richmond on the Port Arthur return trip, this is a good chance to explore it properly_

**Option E: Northern Tasmania Day Trip (ambitious)**

For a longer drive, head north to Launceston. The Queen Victoria Museum is excellent, the Beaconsfield Mine and Heritage Centre is great modern history, and Seahorse World / Platypus World are nearby. Reliquaire in Latrobe is worth a stop if passing through, though maybe not worth a deliberate trip. This is a full day commitment with a lot of driving, so only recommended for those who really want to see the north.
